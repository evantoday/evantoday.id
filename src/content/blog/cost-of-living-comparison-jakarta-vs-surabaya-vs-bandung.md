When it comes to choosing where to live in Indonesia, one of the biggest factors you’ll want to consider is the cost of living. I’ve lived in Jakarta, visited Surabaya, and spent time in Bandung, so I can speak from personal experience about how the costs stack up against each other. 

Let’s break down the costs in these three major cities: Jakarta, Surabaya, and Bandung. Each city has its unique vibe, but they also have their pros and cons regarding expenses. 

## Housing Costs

### Jakarta

Jakarta is the capital and, unsurprisingly, the most expensive city in Indonesia. Rent can be a significant portion of your budget. A one-bedroom apartment in the city center typically costs around IDR 7 million to IDR 12 million per month. If you’re willing to live a bit further out, you might find something in the IDR 4 million to IDR 6 million range.

### Surabaya

Surabaya, on the other hand, is much more affordable when it comes to housing. You can find a one-bedroom apartment in the city center for about IDR 3 million to IDR 5 million per month. If you venture out of the city center, prices can drop even further, sometimes to as low as IDR 2 million.

### Bandung

Bandung falls somewhere in between. Renting a one-bedroom apartment in the center will set you back around IDR 4 million to IDR 8 million. The outskirts are more affordable, with prices starting from IDR 2.5 million.

## Transportation Costs

### Jakarta

Transportation in Jakarta can be a mixed bag. The traffic is notorious, which makes commuting times longer. If you rely on public transport, a monthly pass for the TransJakarta bus system costs around IDR 200,000. If you prefer using Gojek or Grab, a ride across the city can cost anywhere from IDR 20,000 to IDR 100,000, depending on the distance.

### Surabaya

In Surabaya, public transport options are less extensive compared to Jakarta, but it’s generally cheaper. A ride on the city’s bus system is about IDR 5,000 to IDR 10,000. Gojek and Grab fares are also more affordable, averaging around IDR 15,000 to IDR 50,000 for similar distances.

### Bandung

Bandung offers a mix of both worlds. The public transport system is growing, and you can find angkots (shared taxis) for about IDR 5,000 per ride. Gojek and Grab rides can cost between IDR 20,000 to IDR 70,000. 

## Food Costs

### Jakarta

Eating out in Jakarta can vary widely. A meal at a mid-range restaurant usually costs around IDR 100,000, while street food can be as cheap as IDR 20,000. If you’re on a budget, you can manage to eat well for around IDR 1 million to IDR 1.5 million per month.

### Surabaya

Surabaya is known for its delicious and affordable food. You can enjoy a meal at a local warung for as little as IDR 15,000. Dining at a mid-range restaurant will typically cost you about IDR 60,000. I’ve easily kept my monthly food expenses around IDR 800,000 here.

### Bandung

Bandung has a fantastic food scene that offers great value. You can find meals for around IDR 20,000 at local places, while mid-range dining usually costs about IDR 80,000. Monthly food expenses can be managed within IDR 1 million.

## Utility Costs

### Jakarta

Utility bills in Jakarta can be a bit high. For a small apartment, expect to pay around IDR 1 million to IDR 2 million per month for electricity, water, and internet. 

### Surabaya

Utility costs in Surabaya are generally lower, averaging around IDR 800,000 to IDR 1.5 million for similar services.

### Bandung

Bandung’s utility costs are quite similar to Surabaya, usually ranging from IDR 800,000 to IDR 1.5 million per month.

## Lifestyle Considerations

While the numbers above give you an idea of the cost differences, lifestyle choices will also play a significant role. Jakarta offers a fast-paced lifestyle with lots of entertainment and dining options, which can drive up costs. Surabaya is more laid-back, with a friendly atmosphere and a strong local culture. Bandung is famous for its cooler climate and nature, making it a great spot for outdoor enthusiasts.

## Conclusion

In my experience, if you’re looking for a more affordable lifestyle, Surabaya is the best choice. Jakarta is great for those who prioritize career opportunities and a vibrant city life, but expect to pay more for that privilege. Bandung strikes a balance between the two, offering a decent lifestyle without breaking the bank. 

Ultimately, your choice will depend on your personal needs and lifestyle preferences. If you’re considering a move or just curious about the costs, I hope this comparison helps you make a more informed decision.
