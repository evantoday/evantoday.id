## How Much Are Pintu Fees?

Pintu fees are generally made up of trading fees, spread, and withdrawal-related costs depending on the asset and network. The exact amount can change over time, but the important thing is that your real cost is not just the visible fee percentage.

### The fee structure I pay attention to

When I evaluate a crypto app, I don’t stop at the headline fee. I look at three things:

1. **Trading fee** — the explicit fee when you buy or sell.
2. **Spread** — the difference between buy and sell prices, which can quietly increase your cost.
3. **Withdrawal fee** — network or transfer cost when moving crypto out of the app.

That’s where many beginners get surprised. You might think you’re paying “cheap fees,” but if the spread is wide, you’re still losing money on entry and exit.

Here’s a simple way I think about it:

| Cost Type | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Trading fee | Fee charged on buy/sell orders | Affects every transaction |
| Spread | Gap between buy and sell price | Can be the hidden cost |
| Withdrawal fee | Cost to move crypto off-platform | Important if you use your own wallet |

For example, if you buy Rp 1.000.000 of crypto and the total cost impact from fee plus spread is around 1% to 2%, you’re effectively starting with Rp 980.000 to Rp 990.000 of exposure. That’s not terrible, but it matters if you trade often.

My opinion: Pintu is acceptable for occasional buying and holding. If you’re making lots of small trades, fees and spreads become much more important. That’s why I always tell people to think about their strategy first, then pick the app.
