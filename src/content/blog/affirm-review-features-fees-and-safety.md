You're about to check out on Amazon, Walmart, or Shopify, and there it is: "Pay with Affirm." Four easy payments. No hidden fees. Sounds great, right?

I've used Affirm on several purchases and spent months researching how it actually works behind the scenes. The truth is more nuanced than their marketing suggests. Sometimes Affirm is a genuinely good deal. Other times, you're paying 36% APR on a purchase you could have put on a 0% credit card.

Here's my honest, detailed review of Affirm in 2026—the good, the bad, and when you should use something else entirely.

## What Is Affirm?

Affirm is a buy now, pay later (BNPL) service that lets you split purchases into installments. Founded by Max Levchin (PayPal co-founder), it's one of the largest BNPL providers in the US, accepted at over 250,000 merchants including Amazon, Walmart, Target, Peloton, and thousands of Shopify stores.

Unlike traditional credit cards, Affirm shows you the total cost upfront—including interest—before you commit. There are no late fees, no deferred interest, and no compounding interest. What you see is what you pay.

At least, that's the pitch. Let me break down the reality.

## How Affirm Works

### The Checkout Process

1. You select "Pay with Affirm" at checkout
2. Affirm runs a soft credit check (doesn't affect your score)
3. You see your payment options: typically 4 biweekly payments at 0% APR, or 6-36 monthly payments at 0-36% APR
4. You choose a plan and complete the purchase
5. Affirm pays the merchant in full; you pay Affirm in installments

### Payment Options

Affirm offers several payment structures depending on the merchant and your creditworthiness:

**Pay in 4:** Four biweekly payments at 0% APR. Available for purchases typically between $50-1,000. This is Affirm's best product—genuinely interest-free with no catches.

**Monthly payments (0% APR):** Some merchants subsidize the interest, giving you 3, 6, or 12 months at 0%. Common at Peloton, Casper, Purple, and other direct-to-consumer brands.

**Monthly payments (10-36% APR):** For longer terms or less creditworthy borrowers, Affirm charges interest. The rate depends on your credit profile, the merchant, and the loan term.

## What I Like About Affirm

### 1. Transparency

I'll give Affirm credit here—they show you the exact total cost before you commit. No surprises, no fine print traps, no deferred interest (where you suddenly owe all the interest if you miss a payment, like some store credit cards).

### 2. No Late Fees

Affirm doesn't charge late fees. If you miss a payment, they'll pause your ability to make new purchases and may report the delinquency to credit bureaus, but they won't pile fees on top of your balance. This is genuinely better than credit cards.

### 3. No Compounding Interest

Interest is calculated upfront on the original balance (simple interest). With credit cards, interest compounds on your growing balance if you carry it. This makes Affirm's effective cost lower than a credit card at the same APR.

### 4. Pay-in-4 Is Legitimately Free

The four biweekly payment option at 0% APR has no fees, no interest, and no catches. It's essentially a free short-term loan. I've used this for several purchases and it works exactly as advertised.

### 5. Soft Credit Check

Affirm's initial check is a soft pull that doesn't affect your credit score. Only if you're approved and accept a loan with a longer term will Affirm do a hard pull (which has a small, temporary impact on your score).

## What I Don't Like About Affirm

### 1. APR Can Be Extremely High

36% APR is close to predatory lending territory. If you're offered 36%, Affirm has assessed you as a high-risk borrower—and you're paying for it. At that rate, a $500 purchase costs you $603 over 12 months.

### 2. It Encourages Overspending

This is my biggest concern with all BNPL services. Breaking a $400 purchase into four $100 payments makes it feel more affordable, but you're still spending $400. I've caught myself rationalizing purchases I didn't need because the per-payment amount seemed small.

### 3. Credit Bureau Reporting

Affirm reports some loans to Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax. On-time payments can help your credit. But missed payments or defaults will hurt it. And having multiple open BNPL accounts can affect your credit utilization and the number of inquiries.

### 4. Returns Can Be Complicated

If you return an item purchased through Affirm, the refund process adds an extra layer. The merchant processes the return, Affirm adjusts your loan, and you might still need to make payments while waiting for the refund to process. In my experience, this takes 5-10 business days.

### 5. No Rewards or Cashback

Unlike credit cards, Affirm doesn't offer points, miles, or cashback. If you have a credit card with 2% cashback and pay it off monthly, you're actually losing money by using Affirm instead (even at 0% APR, you miss the cashback).

## When to Use Affirm

Based on my experience, here are the situations where Affirm makes sense:

- **Pay in 4 at 0% for planned purchases** — If you were going to buy the item anyway and want to spread the cost over 6 weeks without interest, this is free money management
- **0% APR merchant offers on big purchases** — If Peloton or Casper offers 0% for 12 months through Affirm, take it (assuming you can make the payments)
- **You don't have or can't get a 0% credit card** — Affirm at 0% is better than a credit card at 20%+
- **You want to avoid credit card debt** — Fixed payments with a clear end date can be psychologically easier to manage than revolving credit card balances

## When to Avoid Affirm

- **You're offered 20%+ APR** — At that rate, you're paying significant interest. A credit card with rewards might be cheaper in net terms
- **You're using it to buy things you can't afford** — BNPL makes overspending easier. If you wouldn't buy it with cash, don't buy it with Affirm
- **You have a 0% APR credit card** — The credit card gives you cashback plus credit building. Affirm gives you neither
- **You're making multiple Affirm purchases simultaneously** — Juggling several BNPL payments across different due dates is a recipe for missed payments

## Tips for Using Affirm Responsibly

1. **Always choose the shortest term with the lowest APR** — Pay in 4 at 0% should be your default
2. **Set up autopay immediately** — Don't rely on remembering payment dates
3. **Never use Affirm for impulse purchases** — Sleep on it for 24 hours first
4. **Track all your BNPL commitments** — Add payment dates to your calendar or budget app
5. **Calculate the total cost** — Compare what you'll pay with Affirm vs. buying outright or using a credit card

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Affirm affect your credit score?

It depends. The initial approval uses a soft credit check that doesn't affect your score. However, some Affirm loans (typically longer-term ones) involve a hard credit inquiry and are reported to credit bureaus. On-time payments can help your score, while missed payments will hurt it. Pay-in-4 purchases are generally not reported to credit bureaus.

### Is Affirm safe to use?

Yes, Affirm is a legitimate, publicly traded company (NASDAQ: AFRM) regulated by state and federal lending laws. Your financial data is encrypted, and Affirm uses bank-level security. The risk isn't safety—it's the financial cost of high-APR loans and the behavioral risk of overspending through BNPL.

### What happens if you miss an Affirm payment?

Affirm doesn't charge late fees, which is one of its genuine advantages. However, your account may be suspended from making new purchases, and after extended non-payment, the delinquency may be reported to credit bureaus. Affirm may also send the account to collections. If you're going to miss a payment, contact Affirm proactively—they sometimes offer hardship options.

### Can you pay off Affirm early?

Yes, you can pay off any Affirm loan early with no prepayment penalty. Since Affirm charges simple interest (calculated upfront), paying early on a 0% loan saves you nothing, but paying early on a loan with interest saves you the remaining interest charges. I always recommend paying off Affirm loans as quickly as possible.

### Is Affirm better than a credit card?

It depends on the situation. At 0% APR with Pay in 4, Affirm can be comparable to a credit card—but you miss out on cashback rewards. At higher APRs, a rewards credit card paid off monthly is almost always better. The main advantage of Affirm is the fixed payment structure, which prevents the revolving debt cycle that traps many credit card users. If you struggle with credit card discipline, Affirm's fixed terms can be healthier for your finances.
